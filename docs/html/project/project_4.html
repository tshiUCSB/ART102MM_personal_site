NEO aims to visualize NASA's data for Near-Earth Objects (NEO) in an artistic manner. Each vertical line represents a NEO. The length of the vertical line corresponds to the H (absolute magnitude) of the NEO. The color of the line depends on the minimum orbit intersection distance (moid) to the Earth. A NEO with a moid of less than 0.05 AU is considered a potentially hazardous astroid (PHA) and takes on a more golden or red color, whereas NEOs that have a greater moid have a more violet or blue color. The orbs on the vertical lines move at a speed that corresponds to the orbital period of their respective NEOs.

You can hover your mouse over an individual NEO and view its designationa and discovery date. You can also hold down the mouse button to play NEO. Depending on the type of NEO (apollo, amor, or aten), NEO will use a different oscillator when the scan hits the orb of that NEO. The frequency of the oscillator that plays is contingent on the in-degree of the NEO.
